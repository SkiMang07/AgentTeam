## Artifact type: executive_brief

This deliverable is an executive brief. It is a short, structured document written for a leadership audience that needs to make a decision or understand a situation quickly. It is not a research summary, a chat response, or a long-form explanation.

### Required structure — produce all five sections in this order

**PROBLEM**
One to three sentences. State what the issue, decision point, or situation is. Be specific. Do not restate the task prompt. Do not use "we need to" as an opener — state the problem directly.

**RECOMMENDATION**
One to three sentences. State the specific recommended path forward. Be direct. Lead with the recommendation itself, not the reasoning. The reader should know what you're recommending before they read why.

**RATIONALE**
Two to five sentences. Explain why this recommendation is the right one. Ground it in approved facts and evidence only. Do not invent support. If facts are thin, say so explicitly rather than inflating the reasoning.

**RISKS**
Two to four bullet points. Each bullet is one concrete risk or open question. Keep bullets tight — one risk per bullet, no sub-bullets. If there are no material risks, write a single bullet that says so and explains why.

**NEXT STEPS**
Two to five bullet points. Each bullet is one concrete action. Where possible, include a responsible party and a time horizon. Actions must be completable — not vague aspirations.

---

### Format rules specific to executive briefs

- Use the exact section headers above in bold: **PROBLEM**, **RECOMMENDATION**, **RATIONALE**, **RISKS**, **NEXT STEPS**
- Do not add a title, preamble, or introduction above the PROBLEM section
- Do not add a summary, conclusion, or sign-off after NEXT STEPS
- Prose sections (PROBLEM, RECOMMENDATION, RATIONALE) are plain paragraphs — no bullets
- Bullet sections (RISKS, NEXT STEPS) use plain dashes, not numbered lists
- Total length: aim for 200–400 words. A tight brief is better than a padded one.
- If approved facts don't support a section fully, say so in that section rather than fabricating content
