## Artifact type: decision_memo

This deliverable is a decision memo. It is a short, structured document that records a specific decision — what was decided, why, what alternatives were considered, and what happens next. It is written for an audience that needs to understand or ratify the decision, not to relitigate it. A good decision memo is crisp enough to read in under three minutes.

### Required structure — produce all five sections in this order

**CONTEXT**
Two to four sentences. State the situation that required a decision: what changed, what problem surfaced, or what opportunity was identified. Be specific. Do not editorialize about whether the decision was hard or easy. Do not restate the task prompt.

**DECISION**
One to two sentences. State the decision that was made or is being recommended, in plain language. Use past tense if the decision is already made ("We decided to..."), present tense if it is a recommendation being documented ("The recommendation is to..."). Lead with the decision itself — not the process.

**OPTIONS CONSIDERED**
Two to four bullet points. Each bullet names one option and gives one sentence of context on why it was or was not viable. If only one option was seriously considered, say so and explain why alternatives were ruled out before consideration. Do not pad this section with implausible options.

**RECOMMENDATION**
Two to four sentences. Explain why the chosen option is the right call. Ground it in approved facts and evidence only. If the reasoning depends on assumptions, state them explicitly. Do not invent support — if the facts are thin, say so.

**IMPLICATIONS**
Two to four bullet points. Each bullet states one concrete consequence of the decision: a dependency it creates, a door it closes, a risk it accepts, or a follow-on action it requires. At least one bullet should cover what happens if the decision is reversed or revisited. Keep bullets actionable or specific — not vague.

---

### Format rules specific to decision memos

- Use the exact section headers above in bold: **CONTEXT**, **DECISION**, **OPTIONS CONSIDERED**, **RECOMMENDATION**, **IMPLICATIONS**
- Do not add a title, preamble, or introduction above the CONTEXT section
- Do not add a summary, conclusion, or sign-off after IMPLICATIONS
- Prose sections (CONTEXT, DECISION, RECOMMENDATION) are plain paragraphs — no bullets
- Bullet sections (OPTIONS CONSIDERED, IMPLICATIONS) use plain dashes, not numbered lists
- Total length: aim for 200–350 words. Brevity signals clarity of thinking.
- If approved facts don't support a section fully, say so in that section rather than fabricating content
- Do not use hedge phrases like "it is worth noting" or "one might argue" — state positions directly
